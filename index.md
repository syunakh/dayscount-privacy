# Days Count — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-05-17_

## Summary

DaysCount does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information to its developers or to third parties. All your data lives on your device and in your private iCloud account.

## What we don't collect

- We do not have analytics SDKs.
- We do not have crash-reporting SDKs.
- We do not have advertising SDKs.
- We do not have a backend server.
- We never see your counter titles, dates, notes, or any other content you create.

## What stays on your device

- Counter records (title, notes, dates, mode, icon, milestones, style preferences, reset history).
- Per-app preferences (default style, haptics toggle, first day of week).
- Onboarding completion flag.

## What syncs through iCloud

Counter records sync between your own Apple devices using Apple's CloudKit infrastructure under your personal iCloud account. Apple — not us — is the data processor for that sync. You can disable sync at any time by signing out of iCloud or disabling "iCloud Drive" for DaysCount in iOS Settings → iCloud.

## Notifications

DaysCount asks for permission to send local notifications so it can remind you when a counter hits a milestone (e.g. "30 days since…"). Notifications are scheduled and delivered entirely on your device. No notification token leaves your phone.

## Purchases

DaysCount uses Apple StoreKit to process in-app purchases. Apple — not us — handles the payment and receipt. DaysCount only sees whether a given product ID is owned by the signed-in Apple ID; no payment details ever reach us.

## Children

DaysCount is rated for users aged 4+ and contains no objectionable content, account creation, ads, or third-party links beyond Apple's own systems.

## Changes to this policy

We will update this document if our practices change. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision.
